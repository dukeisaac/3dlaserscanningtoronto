The Evolution of 3D Laser Scanning:

Before delving into Iscano Toronto's groundbreaking contributions, it's essential to understand the evolution of 3D laser scanning. The technology itself has come a long way since its inception. Initially developed for surveying and mapping applications, 3D laser scanning has progressively found its way into fields such as architecture, engineering, construction, and even healthcare.

The fundamental concept behind 3D laser scanning involves the use of lasers to capture the geometry of physical objects or environments. These lasers emit pulses of light that bounce back when they encounter surfaces, creating a precise and detailed point cloud. This point cloud serves as the foundation for generating highly accurate three-dimensional representations of the scanned area.
